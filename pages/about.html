THE COFFEE SHOP

Legend has it that the shepherd Kaldim and his goats were the discoverers of coffee. It happened around the middle of the 9th century in Ethiopia. Goats, after eating the leaves of this plant, became especially frisky, began to frantically run, jump and gallop. What the observant shepherd Kaldim told the abbot of the local monastery. He took a chance and tried the leaves and fruits of the coffee tree himself and, of course, felt the full power of the tonic and stimulating effect ofcoffee.The practical use of the drink was found very quickly: a decoction of coffee did not allow the monks of the monastery to fall asleep during long services. So the tradition of drinking coffee was born. Later, this drink spread throughout the world, and centuries later, everyone can drink their favorite cup in coffee shops.
